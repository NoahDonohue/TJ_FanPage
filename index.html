T.J. McConnell

Floor General McClutchell or "The Man Who Wouldn't Quit"

T.J. celebrates his game winning shot against the New York Knicks

Here's a timeline highlighting TJ's accomplishments:

  • March 25, 1992- Born in Pittsburgh Pennsylvania to esteemed high-school basketball coach Tim McConnell
  • Winter 2010- Averages 34.2 points, 8.2 rebounds, 9.1 assists per game, earned first-team all-state honors, and was named the AP's Pennsylvania Class 3A Player of the year in his senior year
  • June 2010- Graduates from Chartiers Valley High School
  • April 2012- Transferred to Arizona after a sucessful two seasons at Duquesne
  • September 27th, 2015- Signs a contract with the Philadelphia 76ers after a standout performance in the Summer League despite going undrafted
  • October 28th, 2015- Makes NBA debut in the team's season opener against the Boston Celtics
  • February 6th, 2015- Leads the 76ers to a win with 17 points in first career start versus the Brooklyn Nets
  • December 11, 2016- Nearly records a triple-double with 12 points, 10 rebounds, and nine assists in a win over the Detroit Pistons
  • January 6th, 2017- Has career high 17 assists against the rival Boston Celtics
  • January 11th 2017- Hits game-winning buzzer beater over Carmelo Anthony and the New York Knicks with a turnaround jump shot on the baseline
  • February 9th, 2017- Hits game winning shot with 5.8 seconds left against the Orlando Magic
  • February 12, 2018- Recorded first career triple-double in a win over the New York Knicks, finishing with 10 points, 10 rebounds, 11 assists, and 6 steals. First player in franchise history with a triple-double off the bench
  • May 7th, 2018- Scores career high 19 points to lead the Sixers to a Game 4 win over the Boston Celtics in the Conference Semi-Finals
